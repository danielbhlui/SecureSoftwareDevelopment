Reflections

The last assignment of this course requires me to reflect upon critically the events as well as my learnings during this course.

Module Reflection

          A critical incident has happened: I only possess basic level of fluency in Python and now I have to churn out a Python program with such complex topics as timestamp, encryption and checksum? How do I do that? I need a different behavior approach and think deeply about what produces my learning.

          After careful analysis, I have changed my behavior to be more actively learning style by doing more active researches and reading more. I have picked up more Python skills since then.

          My emotional response and analysis. Context and description. After the team work project, I am back to my own, facing the coding exercise alone. I find myself suffering from such symptoms as anxiety, distress and sleep problems. I realize that it is a sign of the pressure is beginning to get to me. I realize a better lifestyle with eating better and more exercise etc. can probably help in the long term, but I don¡¦t have that luxury right now dealing with this short-term crisis. After analyzing about the nature of the task a little more, I realize the Python programming can be challenging at times and timestamp, encryption and checksum are unfamiliar concepts. This can lead to feelings of anxiety and distress. Also, we are only given weeks to compile with the deadlines; this time pressure is adding to the stress. Moreover, there is also personal factor at work here; I confess to there is a lack of experience on me as far as programming in Python is concerned. That is also contributing to anxiety. The evaluation is that while a little stress seems to be able to increase job performance (Kirby and others, 2013), with the symptoms that I am having, it could be reasonabe to believe that my stress level has surpassed at least slightly above the optimal stress level.

          Learning and Changed Actions. My personal philosophy is that more readings, more hands on working on the subject matter, and more thinking and reflections at the end of it¡K would work for me. However, with so much going on and so many things we are supposed to do in this six-week journey, I hardly have time to catch my breath. Kindly excuse me for not having the time to smell the roses along the way. I would definitely want to do more reflection and grow, but I am afraid I simply run out of time. Changed actions. Time management suddenly becomes very material and I have to change myself to appreciate that.
