Based on your reading this week, could you write a section that might be appended to this paper, Salah et al, 2016, which would present the next phase of evolution history, from microservices to the technologies which are commonly in use today?

 

Answers:

Evolution from Microservices to Modern Distributed Technologies

The evolution of distributed systems did not stop with microservices. As technology and business needs continue to evolve, we believe the next phase of the evolution would be the cloud, more specifically, Hybrid and Multi-Cloud Architectures to be exact.

Cloud computing is a low-cost solution. The authors believe that cloud technologies are cost effective in terms of total cost, and they will improve the ratio between maintenance cost and discretionary spending on value added projects. (Mather and others, 2009, p. 227)

Cloud computing offers responsiveness and flexibility. The authors believe that responsiveness and flexibility are two more critical drivers over costs if availability and reliability goals come within acceptable costs. (Mather and others, 2009, p. 227)

The IT expense matches the transaction volumes. Cloud technologies make it for businesses possible to spend more only when it is necessary. The authors suggest that the ability of cloud technologies¡¦ ability to match investment to revenue is an attractive proposition. (Mather and others, 2009, p. 227)

Business users are in direct control of technology decisions. Under cloud computing, business technology users will be able to hire services from a service catalogue only when they need to on demand and they will be in control of the services they use. In such situation it is possible that the IT department will not be involved at all and costs will be directly billed to the business user for transactions and services consumed. Accountability and alignment of IT costs will be improved. (Mather and others, 2009, p. 228)

Hybrid cloud combines on-premises infrastructure with public cloud services, while multi-cloud involves using multiple cloud providers. These architectures offer flexibility and resilience.

Advantages: Avoid vendor lock-in, improved disaster recovery, and optimized cost management.

Challenges: Complexity in management, data consistency, and security.
