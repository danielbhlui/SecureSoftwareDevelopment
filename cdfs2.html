A flowchart of the steps which may have led to the SQL injection weakness occurring

With regards to the question of which UML models I might use to present the design of my proposed software, I would like to use at least class diagram, activity diagram, use case diagram, sequence/interaction diagram and state/machine diagram. This is because these five types of diagrams are the most commonly used UML diagrams in the professional and academic publications. The other seven types of UML diagrams (system sequence diagram, deployment diagram, collaboration/communication diagram, package diagram, object diagram, domain model diagram and component diagram) are not very often used, according to the study done by Koc and others.

 

It is also interesting to note that, within the study, there are 46.1% of publications that use only one UML diagram. There are 18.8% of the publications that use two UML diagrams and there are another 18.8% of the publications that use three UML diagrams. The percentage number of publications that use four UML diagrams are 13.2%. Finally, there are only 3.1% of publications that use five UML diagrams.

 

The study revels that the most commonly used UML diagram in use is the class diagram and the sequence and state machine diagram is not often used. Most of the publications are either conference proceedings or journals and that the articles use UML diagrams mostly for the purposes of designing and modeling.

 

 

Reference

 

Koç, H., Erdoğan, A.M., Barjakly, Y. and Peker, S. (2021) UML diagrams in software engineering research: a systematic literature review. In Proceedings (Vol. 74, No. 1, p. 13). MDPI.
